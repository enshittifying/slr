## üî¥ RED-MARKED DEVIATIONS - Explicitly Contradicts Bluebook/CMOS

### 1. Et al. (Section 15.1)
- ‚ùå **Bluebook**: Use "et al." for 3+ authors
- ‚úÖ **Redbook**: First citation must include **ALL authors**
- Only use "et al." in subsequent supra citations

### 2. Subtitles (Section 15.2)
- ‚ùå **Bluebook**: May omit subtitles
- ‚úÖ **Redbook**: **ALWAYS include subtitles**

### 3. URLs (Section 18.1)
- ‚ùå **Bluebook**: May include both original and archival URLs
- ‚úÖ **Redbook**: Use **ONLY Perma.cc archival link**
- Never include both

### 4. Movies (Section 18.9)
- ‚ùå **Bluebook**: Include production company
- ‚úÖ **Redbook**: Include **director(s)** instead

### 5. Professional Degrees (Section 6.1)
- ‚ùå **CMOS**: JD, PhD (no periods)
- ‚úÖ **Redbook**: **J.D., Ph.D.** (with periods)

### 6. Email (Section 22.3)
- ‚ùå **Bluebook**: "e-mail"
- ‚úÖ **Redbook**: "email" (closed)

### 7. Compound Words (Section 22.3)
- ‚ùå **CMOS**: "decision making" or "decision-making" (context-dependent)
- ‚úÖ **Redbook**: **Always closed**: "decisionmaking", "policymaking", "treatymaking"

### 8. Capitalization After Colon (Section 22.10)
- ‚ùå **CMOS**: Complex rules
- ‚úÖ **Redbook**: Simple rule - Capitalize if:
  - Proper noun, OR

### 9. Federal Register Dates (Section 14.1)

### 10. Statute Short Forms (Section 12.3)

## ‚≠ê SLR-SPECIFIC RULES - Unique to Stanford Law Review

### Parentheticals (Section 1.1)
- **Quoting parentheticals**: ALWAYS required when source quotes another
- **Citing parentheticals**: NEVER required (optional)

### Id. Usage (Section 4.1)
Id. is appropriate in exactly THREE cases:
1. First citation in footnote IF previous footnote cites ONLY that source
2. First/only citation in citation clause IF previous citation cites ONLY that source
3. Within stringcite when citing immediately preceding source

**Not id.-eligible if preceding citation/footnote contains multiple sources**

### Five-Footnote Rule (Section 10.9)
- Count citations in quoting/citing parentheticals
- Count subsequent history
- Count id. references
- Count briefs and court filings

### Signals as Verbs (Section 10.16)
When "see" is used as a verb (not italicized):
- **DO** italicize the case name
- **DO NOT** apply abbreviation rules (BB 10.2.2)
- Example: "For an example, see *Brown v. Board of Education*, 347 U.S. 483 (1954)."

When "see" is a signal in citation clause:
- Use abbreviations
- Example: "Some opinions are well known, *see, e.g.*, Brown v. Bd. of Educ., 347 U.S. 483 (1954)."

### Docket Numbers (Section 10.12)
- Omit initial digit before colon: ~~No. 2:12-cr-02323~~ ‚Üí "No. 12-cr-02323"
- Omit judge initials: ~~No. 99-839-cv (JPO)~~ ‚Üí "No. 99-839-cv"

### Internet Dates/Times (Section 18.4)
- Include all date/time info: (Feb. 1, 2004, 3:56:24 PM CST)
- Convert 24-hour to AM/PM format
- Do not add periods to time zones: PST not P.S.T.
- Never use "last visited" ‚Üí use "(archived [date])"

### Newspaper Articles (Section 16.3)
- If online version available, cite online (not print)
- Use BB 18.2.2 format with RB 18 modifications

### Works in Collections (Section 15.6)
- Always use "in" for all collected works
- Even if originally published elsewhere

## üìù QUOTATIONS (Section 5)

### Block Quotes (Section 5.2)
- 50+ words OR
- Two or more sentences of quoted text

### Omissions (Section 5.3)
- Use three spaced periods for omissions
- Include space before first period
- Include space after last period

### Alterations (Section 5.4)
- Changes requiring bracketed notation
- Silently change: double spaces to single, underlines to italics

### Added Emphasis (Section 1.14)
- Use "(emphasis added)" not "(emphasis mine)"
- Use "(emphasis in original)" not "(emphasis supplied)"

---

## üî¢ NUMBERS IN TEXT (Section 6.5)

- Spell out numbers zero through ninety-nine
- Use numerals for 100 and above
- Exception: Use numerals for dates, page numbers, etc.

## üìö ABBREVIATIONS

### Professional Degrees (Section 6.1)
‚úÖ J.D., Ph.D., LL.M. (with periods)
‚ùå JD, PhD, LLM (no periods)

### Acronyms vs. Initialisms (Section 6.4)
- **Acronym**: Pronounced as word (NASA, NATO)
- **Initialism**: Pronounced as letters (FBI, CIA)

### Introducing (Section 6.3)
- Spell out first use with acronym/initialism in parentheses
- "Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)"

## üíº CASE CITATIONS

### Case Names in Text (Section 1.3)
- Use full case name format when referenced in text
- No v. abbreviation in running text

### Widely Recognized Initials (Section 10.3)
- Use widely known acronyms in case names
- FCC, SEC, NLRB, etc.

### Procedural Phrases (Section 10.4)
- Omit most procedural phrases
- Keep "In re" and "Ex parte"

### Weight of Authority (Section 10.8)
- Order citations by jurisdiction and level of court

## üåê INTERNET SOURCES (Section 18)

### Required Elements
1. Author (if available)
2. Title
3. Website name
4. Date/time (all available info)
5. Perma.cc URL ONLY
6. Archived date (if no other date available)

### Formatting
```
Author, Title, Website (Date, Time Zone), Perma.cc URL.
```

### Special Cases
- Updated content: (updated [date])
- Last modified: (last updated [date])
- No date: Leave blank for ME to fill archival date
