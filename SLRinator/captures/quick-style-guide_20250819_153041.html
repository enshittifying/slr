Quick Style Guide

The following examples illustrate how to cite commonly used sources in accordance with The Bluebook's Whitepages, which are intended for use in law review footnotes.

For citations in court documents and legal memoranda, please refer to the Bluepages.

Cases – rule 10

reporter

Jackson v. Metro. Edison Co., 348 F. Supp. 954, 956–58 (M.D. Pa. 1972), aff’d, 483 F.2d 754 (3d Cir. 1973), aff’d, 419 U.S. 345 (1974).

pending and unreported cases

Albrecht v. Stranczek, No. 87-C9535, 1991 U.S. Dist. LEXIS 5088, at *1 n.1 (N.D. Ill. Apr. 15, 1991).

Jackson v. Virginia, No. 77-1205, slip op. at 3 (4th Cir. Aug. 3, 1978) (per curiam), aff’d, 443 U.S. 307 (1979).

Ross v. Weissman, No. 90-345 (D. Mass. filed Sep. 18, 1990).

Grohman v. Tice, 925 F. 2d 314 (1st Cir. 1991), petition for cert. filed, 60 U.S.L.W. 3422 (U.S. Jan. 14, 1992) (No. 92-212).

court documents

Brief for Appellant at 7, Kitchens v. Scotten, 925 F.2d 314 (1st Cir. 1991) (No. 90-567).

Constitutions – rule 11

U.S. Const. art. I, § 9, cl. 2.

Ark. Const. of 1868, art. III, § 2 (1873).

Statutes – rule 12

code

Administrative Procedure Act § 6, 5 U.S.C. § 555.

22 U.S.C. § 2567 (Supp. I 1983).

session laws

Department of Transportation Act, Pub. L. No. 89-670, § 9, 80 Stat. 931, 944–47 (1966).

National Environmental Policy Act of 1969, Pub. L. No. 91-190, § 102, 83 Stat. 852, 853 (1970) (prior to 1975 amendment).

Legislative Materials – rule 13

unenacted bill

S. 516, 105th Cong. § 2 (1997).

hearing

Transforming the Federal Government to Protect America from Terrorism: Hearing Before the H. Select Comm. on Homeland Sec., 107th Cong. 23–25 (2002) (statement of John Ashcroft, Att’y Gen. of the U.S.).

report

S. Rep. No. 95-797, at 4 (1978), as reprinted in 1978 U.S.C.C.A.N. 9260, 9263.

Administrative Materials – rule 14

FTC Credit Practices Rule, 16 C.F.R. § 444.1 (2019).

Reichhold Chems., Inc., 91 F.T.C. 246 (1978).

Books and Reports – rule 15

books

Deborah L. Rhode, Justice and Gender 56 (1989).

Charles Dickens, Bleak House 49–55 (Normal Page ed., Penguin Books 1971) (1853).

21 Wright & Miller’s Federal Practice & Procedure § 1006 (2d ed. 1987).

pamphlets

Women’s Bureau, U.S. Dep’t of Lab., Leaflet No. 55, A Working Woman’s Guide to Her Job Rights 4 (1978).

works in collection

Kay Deaux & Brenda Major, A Social-Psychological Model of Gender, in Theoretical Perspectives on Sexual Difference 89, 89 (Deborah L. Rhode ed., 1990).

Oliver Wendell Holmes, Law in Science and Science in Law, in Collected Legal Papers 210, 210 (1920).

Periodical Materials – rule 16

consecutively paginated journals

David Rudovsky, Police Abuse: Can the Violence Be Contained?, 27 Harv. C.R.-C.L. L. Rev. 465, 500 (1992).

Thomas R. McCoy & Barry Friedman, Conditional Spending: Federalism’s Trojan Horse, 1988 Sup. Ct. Rev. 85, 100.

Barbara Ward, Progress for a Small Planet, Harv. Bus. Rev., Sep.–Oct. 1979, at 89, 90.

Ellen London, Comment, A Critique of the Strict Liability Standard for Determining Child Support in Cases of Male Victims of Sexual Assault and Statutory Rape, 152 U. Pa. L. Rev. 1957, 1959–63 (2004).

Note, The Death of a Lawyer, 56 Colum. L. Rev. 606 (1956).

book review

Bruce Ackerman, Robert Bork’s Grand Inquisition, 99 Yale L.J. 1419, 1422–25 (1990) (book review).

newspapers

Andrew Rosenthal, White House Tutors Kremlin in How a Presidency Works, N.Y. Times, June 15, 1990, at A1.

Cop Shoots Tire, Halts Stolen Car, S.F. Chron., Oct. 10, 1975, at 43.
