15.6
Prefaces, Forewords, Introductions, and Epilogues
(p.160)
Cite a preface, foreword, introduction, or epilogue by someone other than the author as follows:
L. Maria Child,
Introduction
to
Harriet A. Jacobs
, Incidents in the Life of a Slave Girl
3, 3–4 (L. Maria Child & Jean F. Yellin eds., Harvard Univ. Press 1987) (1861).
Henry M. Hart & Herbert Wechsler,
Preface to the First Edition
of
Paul M. Bator et al., Hart and Wechsler’s the Federal Courts and the Federal System
, at xxvii, xxx (3d ed. 1988).
Cite to similar material by the author of the work without special designation:
John Hart Ely, Democracy and Distrust
, at vii (1980).
