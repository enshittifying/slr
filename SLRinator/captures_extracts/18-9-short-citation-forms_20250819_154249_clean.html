18.12 Short Citation Forms (pp. 203–04)

(a) Internet.

When citing to previously referenced web-based sources or digital media content, use a “supra” form with the last name of the author, if any, or the title or description of the document. “Id.” may also be used in accordance with rule 4. If various subsections of a web-based source or digital media content with no author are cited or if more than one posting by a given author is cited, include the subsection title. A URL need not be repeated after a full citation:

1 See S.A. Karpukhin, On Silence, Ploughshares: Blog (Dec. 1, 2022), https://pshares.org/blog/on-silence/%C2%A0 [https://perma.cc/32LB-7NMC].

2 Pfizer, Inc., http://www.pfizer.com (on file with the Yale Law Journal) (last visited July 17, 2024).

3 Karpukhin, supra note 1; Pfizer, Inc., supra note 2.

(b) Commercial electronic databases.

For materials available on an electronic database, use a unique database identifier, if one has been assigned, in constructing a short form:

Int’l Snowmobile Mfrs. Ass’n v. Norton, No. 00-CV-229-B, 2004 WL 2337372, at *3 (D. Wyo. Oct. 14, 2004).

becomes:

Int’l Snowmobile, 2004 WL 2337372, at *3.

Chavez v. Metro. Dist. Comm’n, No. 3:02CV458, 2004 U.S. Dist. LEXIS 11266, at *5 n.3 (D. Conn. June 1, 2004).

becomes:

Chavez, 2004 U.S. Dist. LEXIS 11266, at *5 n.3.

(c) Electronic storage media and microform.

When citing a separately published document available on an electronic storage device or microform, use the short form appropriate for the original document; it is not necessary to indicate the storage source once it has been given in the first full citation. “Id.” and “supra” may be used according to rule 4.

(d) Films, broadcasts, audio recordings, and works of visual art.

When citing films, broadcasts, audio recordings, photographs, and illustrations, “id.” and “supra” may be used according to rule 4:

4 Nightline: Microsoft Monopoly (ABC television broadcast, aired Apr. 3, 2000) (transcript on file with the Columbia Law Review).

5 Id.

6 See id.; Meat Loaf, For Crying out Loud, on Bat out of Hell (Apple Music, Epic Recs. Oct. 21, 1977).

7 Meat Loaf, supra note 6; see also Nightline: Microsoft Monopoly, supra note 4 (describing Microsoft’s response to the court ruling).

(e) Social media and communication.

When citing a previously referenced social media post (including federated social media and reposts), a “supra” form may be used with the name (if available) and the handle of the poster according to rule 4. Likewise, "id." may be used according to rule 4:

11 u/orhazelw, supra note 10.

14 Anu Bradford (@anubradford), supra note 12.

For audio and video communications, also omit the other parties present in the call, but keep the identification of the individual whose statement is cited:

95 Statement by Cain Marko, supra note 87.

(f) Hardware and software.

When citing a previously referenced hardware device, a “supra” form may be used with the model name according to rule 4. Likewise, “id.” may be used according to rule 4:

108 GeForce RTX 4090 Founders Edition, supra note 100.

When citing a previously referenced software, a “supra” form may be used with the name and version of the software (if available) according to rule 4. Likewise, “id.” may be used according to rule 4:

120 Discord, ver. 235, supra note 116.
