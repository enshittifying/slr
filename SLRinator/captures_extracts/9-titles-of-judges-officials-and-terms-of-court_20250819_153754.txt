9
Titles of Judges, Officials, and Terms of Court
(p.99)
(a) Justices and judges.
Justices are referred to as “
Justice Gorsuch
” and “
Chief Justice Roberts
.” Parenthetical references are to “
Breyer, J.,
” “
Kagan, J.,
” and “Breyer
& Kagan, JJ.
” Judges are referred to as “
Judge Readler
” and “
Chief Judge Smith
.” First names are not used for judges, unless a court has two individuals with the same last name, in which case the first name should be included on first reference. Capitalize “
justice
,” “
chief justice
,” and “
judge
”
according to
rule 8(d)(ii)
. For parenthetical references, abbreviate titles of judges and officials as indicated in
table T11
.
(b) Ordering.
As a matter of etiquette, lists of judges should be in the order indicated at the beginning of each volume of the official reporter for the court. Justices of the United States Supreme Court are always listed with the Chief Justice first and then in order of seniority.
(c) Term of court.
A term of court currently in progress may be referred to as “
this term
.” The immediately preceding term, no longer in progress at the time of publication, should be referred to as “
last term
.” Any term may be indicated by year:
the 1999 term
The Supreme Court Term should be indicated by the year in which the Term began, not the year it ended. Capitalize “
term
” according to
rule 8(d)(ii)
.
