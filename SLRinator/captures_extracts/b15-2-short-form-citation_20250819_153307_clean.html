B15.2 Short Form Citation (p.24)

Use “id.” to refer to a book or other nonperiodical material cited in the immediately preceding citation. Otherwise, use “supra.” The “supra” form consists of: (1) the author’s last name; (2) “supra,” underlined or italicized up to but not including the comma; and (3) a new pincite:

Full Cite Id. Cite Supra Cite
Richard Posner, How Judges Think 269 (2010). Id. at 204–05. Posner, supra, at 204–05.
Mary L. Dunnewold et al., Judicial Clerkships: A Practical Guide 77 (2010). See id. at 80. See Dunnewold et al., supra, at 80.
Fleming James, Jr. & Geoffrey C. Hazard, Jr., Civil Procedure § 2.35 (5th ed. 2001). Id. § 1.7. James & Hazard, supra, § 1.7.
