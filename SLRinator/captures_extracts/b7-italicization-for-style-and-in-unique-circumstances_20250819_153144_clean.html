B7 Italicization for Style and in Unique Circumstances (p.10)

Words and phrases may be italicized (or underscored) for emphasis.

You may italicize (or underscore) non-English words and phrases unless they have been incorporated into common English usage. Most Latin words and phrases that are used in legal writing are in common English usage. Obsolete or uncommon Latin words and phrases, however, should be italicized (or underscored).

“Id.” is always italicized (or underscored).

For more information about italicizing for style, see rule 7.
