T2.12 Egypt, Arab Republic of

(Civil Law)

Egypt is a civil law jurisdiction. Article 2 of the 2014 Egyptian Constitution states that the principles of Islamic Sharia are the main source of legislation.

Cases

Judicial power is vested in three separate court systems: the Supreme Constitutional Court with jurisdiction over constitutional matters; the State Council with jurisdiction over administrative cases; and the regular court system with jurisdiction over civil and criminal matters. In addition, the military has its own court system with jurisdiction pertaining to military criminal affairs.

Constitutions

Constitution of the Arab Republic of Egypt, 18 Jan. 2014.

Egypt has had a series of written constitutional documents, beginning with the 1923 foundational instrument. The different constitutions must be cited to a consolidated text incorporating the revisions and substantial reforms adopted. For example, Constitution of the Arab Republic of Egypt, 11 Sept. 1971, as amended, 22 May 1980, 25 May 2005, 26 Mar. 2007.

An unofficial English translation of the current Constitution, enacted in January 2014, is available at https://www.sis.gov.eg/Newvr/Dustor-en001.pdf.

The Official Gazette

The Official Gazette (al-Jarīdah al-Rasmīyah) and its accompanying publication (al-Waqā’i‘ al-Misrīyah) are the authoritative sources for laws and presidential and ministerial decrees. Earlier laws should be cited to the relevant government newspaper of the time, as follows:
