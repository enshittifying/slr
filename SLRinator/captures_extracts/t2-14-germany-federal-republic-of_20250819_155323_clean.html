Constitution

Grundgesetz für die Bundesrepublik Deutschland (Basic Law) Grundgesetz GG

The Basic Law is subdivided into articles.

When citing the Basic Law for the first time within a work, provide the link to the up-to-date vernacular text or to a recent translation as provided by Gesetze im Internet, http://www.gesetze-im-internet....

Grundgesetz [GG] [Basic Law], translation at http://www.gesetze -im-internet.de/englisch_gg/index.html.

Cite amending laws to the Basic Law like statutes:

Gesetz zur Änderung des Grundgesetzes [Law Amending Basic Law], Nov. 15, 2019, BGBl I at 1546 (Ger.).

Codes

Citation format: <code name> [<code abbreviation>] [<English translation of code name>], <“as amended,” or amending law with citation, if applicable>, <relevant subdivisions, if desired> <online version in German or English, if available> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

It is possible to cite to a sentence or clause within the section, paragraph, or sentence number. Cite the section before paragraphs or sentence numbers.

Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch [BGB] [Civil Code], § 793, para. 2, sentence 2, http://www.gesetze-im-internet... (Ger.).

Handelsgesetzbuch [HGB] [Commercial Code], § 8, http://www.gesetze-im-internet... (Ger.).

The Sozialgesetzbuch (SGB) (Social Code) is subdivided into twelve books that are described by a Roman numeral (number thirteen was omitted, the last book is number fourteen). For instance, Sozialgesetzbuch V Gesetzliche Krankenversicherung deals with social health insurance.

Citation format: <statute or decree full name or, if available and part of the statutory title, short title> [<abbreviated statute or decree name, if available>] [<English translation of short title>], <date of enactment>, <name of gazette of promulgation> [<abbreviation of gazette of promulgation>] at <volume, page, and/or sentence number of gazette, if desired>, <“as amended,” or amending law with citation, if applicable>, <relevant subdivisions, if desired> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Statutes are often amended by subsequent legislation. In these cases, citing the original enactment does not indicate what amendments have since occurred. Add “as amended,” or cite a particular amending law. Add a translation of the statute name if helpful and cite an English translation of the statute if available, or provide a link to the consolidated online version, if available.

Aktiengesetz [AktG] [Stock Corporation Act], Sept. 6, 1965, BGBl I at 1089, last amended by Gesetz [G], July 17, 2017, BGBl I at 2446, art. 9 (Ger.), https://www.gesetze-im-interne....

If statutes are revised to provide a completely updated text after their initial enactment, cite the first enactment, followed by the date and citation of the revised version (Neufassung).

Bundesnotarordnung [Federal Act on Notaries Public], Feb. 13, 1937, RGBl II at 191, revised Feb. 24, 1961, BGBl I at 97 (Ger.).

Legislative materials

There are two federal legislative bodies: the Deutscher Bundestag (BT) (Federal Parliament) and the Deutscher Bundesrat (BR) (Federal Council). Cite published Drucksachen (literally "publications," i.e., legislative materials) by legislative period and number for BT and by number and last two digits of year for BR (optionally add link to online version).

Regierungsentwurf [Cabinet Draft], Deutscher Bundestag: Drucksachen[BT]  17/13423, http://dip21.bundestag.de/dip2... /17/134/1713423.pdf (Ger.).

Regierungsentwurf [Cabinet Draft], Deutscher Bundesrat: Drucksachen [BR] 257/06 (Ger.).

Treaties and conventions

Cite treaties and conventions to BGBl II, and use the citation format for statutes.

T2.14.1 – German Länder

Cases

In the integrated German court system, the trial and appellate courts are state courts, and the courts of last resort are federal courts. These courts adjudicate matters of federal and state law.

Constitutional jurisdiction

Staatsgerichtshof (StGH) or Verfassungsgerichtshof (e.g., BayVerfGH for Bavaria) (constitutional court that adjudicates matters relating to the state’s constitution): Cite to the official reporter, a periodical, or a website.

BayVerfGH July 19, 2007, 60 Sammlung von Entscheidungen des Bayerischen Verwaltungsgerichtshofs mit Entscheidungen des Bayerischen Verfassungsgerichtshofs [BayVGH (NF)] 151 (Bavaria) (Ger.).

Constitutions

Constitutions (Verfassung) of the German states (Länder) are designated as such, followed by the name of the state in question:

Verfassung Bayern Verf Bay

Statutes and decrees

Cite a Landesgesetz (LG) (state statute) or Landesverordnung (LVO) (state administrative regulation) to the law gazette (GVBl.) of the individual state.

Bayerisches Verfassungsschutzgesetz, repromulgated Apr. 10, 1997, Bayerisches Gesetz- und Verordnungsblatt [Bay GVBl] 70 (Bavaria) (Ger.).

Bayerisches Gesetz- und Verordnungsblatt Bay GVBl

Internet sources

Länder laws and regulations, as well as decisions of the Länder courts, are available on their official websites. For instance, in the Land of North Rhine-Westphalia, the Ministry of Justice provides a legal library that contains the court decisions of North Rhine-Westphalia as well as the most important laws of the Federation and the other Länder. The portal is available in English at http://www.justiz.nrw.de/WebPo..., but the sources are in German.
