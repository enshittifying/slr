T2.18
India
(Common Law)
India is a constitutional republic with a federal structure. India’s Parliament is bicameral: consisting of the House of the People (Lok Sabha) and the Council of States (Rajya Sabha). Indian states have their own legislative assemblies. India has a union judiciary, consisting of the Supreme Court and various High Courts.
English is the primary language of the legal system of India. Publication of cases, laws, regulations, and reporting systems are all in English.

Internet Sources
For citations to an internet source, use
rule 18
, and preferably cite to
indiacode.nic.in
. Legislation made available on
https://indiacode.nic.in
is not regularly updated. Reference should be made to legislation available on legal databases such as Manupatra and SCCOnline. If possible, verify versions available against the Gazette.

Reports and Other Documents
Citations to reports of parliamentary committees should include the name, session, and other details of the parliament committee.
Citation format:
<government department or committee name>, <report title>, <report number, if any>,  <session year>, <abbreviation of applicable House of Parliament where report was issued> <paper number>, <paragraph symbol and number, or “at” page number of specific material>
(
<jurisdiction abbreviation if not evident from context>
).
Public Accounts Committee (2023-24), Manpower and Logistics Management in Delhi Police (140th Report, 17th Lok Sabha).
Citations to reports of expert commissions or committees set up by the government should be cited using
rule 15
.
Internet sources
The latest status of court cases that are either pending or disposed of, and judgments and orders issued by the Supreme Court or any state High Court can be found at
https://main.sci.gov.in/case-status
.
Judgment Information System of the Supreme Court of India provides free access to all judgments of the Supreme Court and certain High Courts, as well as the SCR journal from 1950 to present at
https://main.sci.gov.in/judgments
.
The text of primary and delegated legislation pending or passed by the Parliament, state legislatures, and central and state governments can be found online. The Gazette of India and gazettes for some states can be assessed online at
http://egazette.nic.in
. Several states also have separate websites for the e-gazettes.
Parliamentary bills, including texts, and other enactments may also be searched and located on the website of the Government of India at
https://mpa.gov.in
.
Legislative documents, including statutes, bills, legislative history, etc. can also be found at the website of PRS Legislative Research at
https://prsindia.org
.
