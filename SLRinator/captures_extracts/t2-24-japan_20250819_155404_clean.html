T2.24 Japan

(Civil Law)

Romanization

For romanization, word division, and capitalization of Japanese words, apply the rules of the American Library Association and the Library of Congress: Use the modified Hepburn romanization system adopted by Japanese-English dictionaries, such as Kenkyūsha shin Wa-Ei daijiten (Kenkyūsha’s New Japanese-English Dictionary). Indicate long vowels with a macron as in “hōrei” or “hanreishū.” Add an apostrophe between ‘n’ and a vowel, and ‘n’ and ‘y’asin“gen’in,”or“kin’yū,”toclarifythemeaningofwords.Fordetails,seethe Library of Congress ALA-LC Romanization Tables at http://www.loc.gov/ catdir/cpso/roman.html.

Citation format: <law name> [<law abbreviation, if different from full name>] [<English translation of law abbreviation or name>], <number> of <year>, <article, paragraph, number, if applicable> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).
