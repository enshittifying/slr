T2.27
Malaysia
(Civil/Islamic Law)
Malaysia operates two legal systems simultaneously—civil law and Syariah law. Civil laws are based on the common law tradition and are applicable to all persons. In addition, Syariah law covers personal and family matters applicable to Muslims.

Civil cases:
The most commonly-cited reporters in Malaysia are the Malayan Law Journal (MLJ) and the Current Law Journal (CLJ). Both MLJ and CLJ provide reports of cases from the Federal Court, the Court of Appeal and High Courts.
Cubic Electronics Sdn Bhd (In Liquidation) v. Mars Telecommunications Sdn Bhd
[2019] 6 MLJ 15, [30] (Federal Court).
Syariah cases:
CLJ provides reports of Syariah Court decisions in Malaysia starting from 2004. The reporter is abbreviated as “
CLJ(Sya)
” prior to 2014 and as “
SHR
” 2014 onwards.
Ismail Yahya v Ketua Pendakwa Syarie Terengganu
[2015| 1 SHR 97.
Constitution
Malaysia’s Federal Constitution should be cited as follows:
Fed. Const.
, art. 8(5)(a) (Malay.).
Statutes
Malaysian statutes consist of federal Acts and state Enactments. Civil laws are enacted in federal Acts, whereas Syariah laws are enacted in state Enactments. State laws enacted in the state of Sarawak are known as Ordinances. Current federal Acts and relevant subsidiary legislation are available online at
https://lom.agc.gov.my
.

Parliamentary Debates
The Malaysian Parliament is a bicameral system which consists of the Dewan Rakyat (House of Representatives) and the Dewan Negara (Senate). The parliamentary debates (Hansard) for both assemblies are only published in the Malay language, at
https://www.parlimen.gov.my
.
