T2.28 Mexico

(Civil Law)

Cases

Mexico does not have an official system of citation for cases decided by courts at the federal level. It is customary to cite federal cases provided in the Court Reporter's digital system in the following format:

Citation format for cases decided by the Mexican Supreme Court and federal courts: <rubro>, <court name> [<court abbreviation>] <reporter name> <Época>, Tomo <volume number> <month and year of reporter> <number of decision in reporter> <case number> página <first page of decision> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Personalidad. En contra de la resolución que dirime esta cuestión, previamente al fondo, procede el amparo indirecto, Pleno de la Suprema Corte de Justicia [SCJN], Semanario Judicial de la Federación y su Gaceta, Novena Época, Tomo XIII, Enero de 2001, Tesis P./J. 4/2001, página 11 (Mex.).

The "rubro," or "case title" in English, is a short statement prepared by a court on the topic and issue discussed in the case.  "Épocas" or "time periods" in English, are periods of time determined by the Supreme Court of Mexico in order to organize cases by chronological order.  As of 2021, the Supreme Court was in the eleventh época.  The first four épocas are no longer in force.  The last seven épocas (from the fifth through the eleventh) include cases that are currently in force.  All cases decided by federal courts indicate the época in which the case was decided.

Federal court structure

The Suprema Corte de Justicia de la Nación (SCJN) is the highest court of constitutional and federal jurisdiction in Mexico and sits as a full court (pleno) or in chambers. The Tribunales Colegiados de Circuito (TCC) are the equivalents, to some extent, of the U.S. federal courts of appeals.

 Case law publications

Cases decided by federal courts in Mexico are currently published in the Semanario Judicial de la Federación y su Gaceta (SJFG) (Federal Judicial Weekly Report and its Gazzette). Only cases decided by SCJN, PC, and the TCC are published in the SJFG. The SJFG volumes are published monthly.

The Mexican government publishes legislative and executive materials in the Diario Oficial de la Federación (DOF), which is the official gazette of the federal government in Mexico.  The DOF provides ready access to the official text of federal laws, presidential documents, administrative regulations and notices, treaties, and descriptions of federal organizations, programs, and activities. Some administrative orders issued by the Supreme Court are published in the DOF as well. Cite to DOF for all federal legislation.

Books

Citation format: <last name of author>,<first name of author>,<title of book>,<edition>, <location of publication>: <publisher>, <year of publication>, <volume/ tome number or name of series or collection, if applicable>, p.<page number>.

Acosta Romero, Miguel, Teoría general del derecho administrativo, 17ª. ed., México: Porrúa, 2004, p. 321.

To cite a chapter in a book, insert the title of the chapter and "en" before the title of the book.

Acosta Romero, Miguel, Títulos valores, en: Derecho bancario, México, Porrúa, 2013, p 219.

If a book has four or more authors, place "et al." after the name of the first author.

Acosta Romero, Miguel, "et al," Tratado de sociedades mercantiles con énfasis en la sociedad anónima: México, 2ª ed., México, Porrúa, 2004, p. 169.

If a book has been translated, place the name of the translator after the edition.

Acosta Romero, Miguel, Teoría general del derecho administrativo, 17ª. ed., trad. de Jorge Ochoa Tena, México: Porrúa, 2004, p. 321.
