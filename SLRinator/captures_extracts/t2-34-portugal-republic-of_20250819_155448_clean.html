(Civil Law)

Cases

Citation format for decisions published in the official gazette (Diário da República): <court name> [<English translation of court name>], Precedent no. <Precedent no., if a precedent decision (assento)> of <date of decision in day-month-year format>, Proceedings no. <docket no.>, DR <gazette publication number> Series <series number> of <date of gazette publication in day-month-year format>, <first page>, <URL of online publication on gazette website, if available> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Supremo Tribunal de Justiça [Supreme Court of Justice], Precedent no. 2/2000 of 09-12-1999, Proceedings no. 298/99, DR 31 Series 1-A of 07-02-2000, 468, https://dre.pt/application/dir/pdf1sdip/2000/02/031A00/04680471.pdf (Port.).

Citation format for decisions published in a reporter or periodical: <court name> [<English translation of court name>] of <date of decision in day-month-year format> in Proceedings no. <docket no.>, <reporter or periodical name or abbreviation> <volume number, if applicable> (<for periodicals, year of publication of volume>), <first page>, available at <authoritative website, if available> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Supremo Tribunal de Justiça [Supreme Court of Justice] of 19-10-2000 in Proceedings no. 87/2000, BMJ 500 (2000), 5, available at http://www.dgsi.pt (Port.).

Citation format for unpublished decisions: <court name> [<English translation of court name>] of <date of decision in day-month-year format> in Proceedings no. <docket no.> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>), available at <authoritative website, if available>.

Tribunal da Relação de Coimbra [Court of Appeal of Coimbra] of 11-13-2019 in Proceedings No. 2519/18.8T8LRA.C1 (Port.), available at http://www.dgsi.pt.

Spell out the full name of the court and its English translation the first time citing any case; afterward, abbreviations may be used.

Ordinary jurisdiction

Supremo Tribunal de Justiça (STJ) [Supreme Court of Justice] (court of last resort for civil and criminal matters): Cite to the official gazette (Diário da República); if unpublished in the official gazette, cite to the online database of the Ministry of Justice (http://www.dgsi.pt) or to a reporter or periodical.

Tribunal da Relação de <place of seat> (TR <first letter of place of seat> e.g., TRL, for the court of Lisboa) [Court of Appeal of <place of seat>] (the country is divided into five judicial districts, each with an appellate court with jurisdiction over civil and criminal matters; appellate courts are located in Coimbra, Évora, Guimarães, Lisboa, and Porto): Cite to the online database of the Ministry of Justice (http://www.dgsi.pt) or to a reporter or periodical.

There are no official reporters for lower court decisions. While open to public access, lower court decisions are usually only available by consultation or request from the relevant court.

Constitutional jurisdiction

Tribunal Constitucional (TC) [Constitutional Court]: Cite to the official gazette (Diário da República); if unpublished in the official gazette, cite to the online court database http://www.tribunalconstitucional.pt) or to a reporter or periodical.

Administrative jurisdiction

Supremo Tribunal Administrativo (STA) [Supreme Administrative Court] (court of last resort for administrative law matters): Cite to the official gazette (Diário da República); if unpublished in the official gazette, cite to the online database of the Ministry of Justice (http://www.dgsi.pt) or to a reporter or periodical.

Tribunal Central Administrativo Norte (TCAN) [Central Administrative Court North] (appellate court with jurisdiction over administrative law matters in the northern district): Cite to the online database of the Ministry of Justice (http://www.dgsi.pt) or to a reporter or periodical.

Tribunal Central Administrativo Sul (TCAS) [Central Administrative Court South] (appellate court with jurisdiction over administrative law matters in the southern district): Cite to the online database of the Ministry of Justice (http://www.dgsi.pt) or to a reporter or periodical.

There are no official reporters for lower administrative court decisions. While open to public access, lower courts decisions are usually only available by consultation or request from the relevant court.

State audit jurisdiction

Tribunal de Contas [Audit Court]: cite to the online court database (http://www.tcontas.pt).

Constitution

Constituição da República Portuguesa [Constitution] C.R.P.

When citing the Constitution for the first time, spell out the name of the Constitution in full and provide the link to an up-to-date vernacular text or translation, as provided by the website of the official gazette (Diário da República).

Constituição da República Portuguesa [C.R.P.], English translation available at https://dre.pt/constitution-of-the-portuguese-republic.

The Constitution is divided into parts and titles, and subdivided into articles, numbers, and paragraphs. Do not mention a part or a title unless referring to it as a whole. When referring to a particular division or subdivision abbreviate as follows.

C.R.P., art. 6, no. 1.

C.R.P., art. 9, para. b).

C.R.P., Part 1, Title II.

Cite the Constitution currently in force without a date. If the cited provision has been amended or repealed, either indicate parenthetically the fact and year of amendment or repeal or cite the amending or repealing Constitutional Act (Lei Constitutional ) in full.

C.R.P., art. 10 (repealed 1982).

C.R.P., art. 140, amended by Lei Constitutional n. ̊ 1/82 de 30 de setembro [Constitutional Act no. 1/82 of September 30th].

Portugal has had six constitutions since its first in 1822. The current constitution was adopted in 1976. When citing a previous constitution, use the following citation format instead: <Constitution Name> de <year of adoption> [<year of adoption> <English translation of constitution name>] <URL of online version in Portuguese or English, if available> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Carta Constitucional da Monarquia Portuguesa de 1826 [1826 Constitutional Charter of the Portuguese Monarchy], https://digitarq.arquivos.pt/details?id=4161652 (Port.).

Statutes and regulations

Citation format: <statute or regulation category in Portuguese> n. ̊ <statute or regulation number> de <day of publication in official gazette> de <full name in Portuguese of month of publication in official gazette> [<English translation of statute or regulation category> no. <statute or regulation number> of <day of publication in official gazette> <full name in English of month of publication in official gazette>], <URL of online publication on gazette website> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Lei n. ̊ 49/95 de 30 de Agosto [Act no. 49/95 of 30 August], https://dre.pt/application/conteudo/547979 (Port.).

Decreto-Lei n. ̊ 1/2020 de 9 de janeiro [Decree-Law no. 1/2020], https://dre.pt/application/conteudo/127899795 (Port.).

Decreto Legislativo Regional n. ̊ 30/2019/A de 28 de novembro [Regional Legislative Decree no. 30/2019/A of 28 November], https://dre.pt/application/conteudo/126669989 (Port.).

Note that n. ̊ stands for “número.” Note also that the second two numbers in the statute or regulation number indicate the year; for example, Lei n. ̊ 49/95 de 30 de Agosto was enacted in 1995.

There are various categories of statutes and regulations. Statutes can take the form of Act (Lei), Decree-Law (Decreto-Lei), or Regional Legislative Decree (Decreto Legislativo Regional), according to whether they are issued by Parliament, Cabinet, or Governments of any of the two Autonomous Regions (Azores and Madeira), respectively. Common categories of regulations include Decree (Decreto), Regulatory Decree (Decreto-Regulamentar), Ordinance (Portaria), and Order (Despacho).

Statutes and regulations are subdivided into articles, numbers, and paragraphs. When referring to a particular subdivision abbreviate as follows:

Decreto-Lei n. ̊ 161/98 de 24 de junho [Decree-Law no. 161/98 of 24 June], art. 3, no. 2, https://dre.pt/application/conteudo/478433.

If the cited provision has been amended, cite the amending statute in full and insert the URL of the online publication of each of the amended and amending statutes:

Lei n. ̊ 23/2006 de 23 de junho [Act no. 23/2006 of 23 June], art. 16, no. 4, https://dre.pt/application/conteudo/359360, amended by Lei n. ̊ 57/2019 de 7 de agosto [Act no. 57/2019 of 7 August], https://dre .pt/application/conteudo/123770987.

Statutes and regulations are often republished in a consolidated version when amendments are made. Unless only previous versions are pertinent, when citing such statutes and regulations, include a reference to the most recent republication.

Lei n. ̊ 6/1997 de 1 de março [Act no. 6/1997 of 1 March], republished by Lei n. ̊ 36/2012 de 27 de Agosto [Act no. 36/2012 of 27 August], https://dre.pt/application/conteudo/174561.

Legislative materials

Both Parliament (Assembleia da República) and Cabinet (Conselho de Ministros) hold legislative power. However, only Parliament publishes legislative materials. Cite published legislative materials to Parliament’s gazette (Diário da Assembleia da República).
