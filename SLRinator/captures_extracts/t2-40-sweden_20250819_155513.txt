T2.42
Sweden
(Civil Law)
Swedish constitutions, codes, and statutes are usually divided into chapters, and are always organized by sections (§). Note that a section in Swedish is called
paragraf
, which may cause some confusion.

Ordinary jurisdiction
Courts of ordinary jurisdiction include:
Högsta Domstolen
(HD) (Supreme Court), cite to
Nytt Juridiskt Arkiv
(NJA) (Supreme Court Reports);
Hovrätt
(HovR) (Court of Appeals), cite to Rättsfall från Hovrätterna (RH) (cases from the Courts of Appeal); and
Tingsrätt
(TR) (district courts, courts of first instance in civil and criminal matters), citation should include T for civil cases (Tvistemål) and B for criminal cases (Brottmål).

Codes
The National Code of Sweden of 1734 is still formally in use but is not a systematic codification as is, for example, the Civil Code of France. The National Code serves to collect and organize laws on the same legal topic, but many statutes fall outside the scope of the National Code.
