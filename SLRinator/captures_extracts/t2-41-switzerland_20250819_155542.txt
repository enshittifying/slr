To cite a former version of a code that was in effect at an earlier date, rather than citing to the updated and systematic compilation of laws, which is maintained as a loose-leaf system and available online, it is preferable to cite to the chronological collection of laws and to mention the operative date or the last amending law:
Schweizerisches Strafgesetzbuch [StGB] [Criminal Code]
Dec. 21, 1937, SR 757 (1938),
as amended by
Gesetz, Oct. 4, 1991, AS 2465 (1992), art. 37, para. 1 (Switz.).
Swiss codes and statutes are subdivided into articles (not sections) and thereafter into paragraphs and/or numbers. When new articles are inserted into an act, they are often numbered as follows:
art. 264
,
art. 264a
,
art. 264b
, etc. Until recently, this numbering looked like this:
art. 179
,
179bis
,
179ter
, up to
art. 179novies
. In some English translations, this numbering has been replaced by
art. 179
,
art. 179a
, etc. This substitution appears permissible; it may be preferable, however, to give both versions:
StGB
art. 179bis (
StGB
art. 179a)

Internet sources
The most comprehensive electronic source is the Federal Authorities of the Swiss Confederation website at
http://www.admin.ch
. This site has a portal in English that links to the Parliament, the Executive Branch of Government, and to the four federal courts. Legislation, legislative materials, and cases are available in the three or four national languages (some documentation is also in Rumantsch and English).
http://www.bger.ch
(Federal Court): Three main files are available to the public without subscription. The BGE/ATF/DTF file contains published decisions beginning in 1954 and is best searched in the Index by BGE/ATF/ DTF citation. The file for additional judgments (weitere Urteile ab 2000/ Autres arrêts dès 2000/Altre decisioni dal 2000) contains unpublished decisions and is best searched by docket number. The file for new decisions (Liste der Neuheiten/Liste des dernières décisions/Elenco delle ultime decisioni) contains the decisions of the last two weeks and is best searched by date.

Internet sources
The websites of the cantons are accessible from the Swiss service portal at
https://www.ch.ch/en/addresses-administrative-authorities
. The link labeled “Addresses of administrative authorities” takes you to a search menu from which you can search for a specific canton’s website. The cantonal websites are in the language(s) spoken in the canton. For instance, the website of the Canton of Aargau, at
http://www.ag.ch
, is in German. From there, click on “Gesetze” to access the systematic compilation of Aargau legislation in German. The cantonal pages differ slightly, so an understanding of the language is necessary to navigate and link to the appropriate files.
