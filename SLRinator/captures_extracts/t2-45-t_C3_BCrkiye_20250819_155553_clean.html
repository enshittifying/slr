Constitutional Court

Citation format: <court name> [<abbreviation of court name>] [<English translation of court name>], <date of decision>, <matter number> <decision number>, <author of the individual opinion, if applicable>, <paragraph number, if applicable>, <optionally, URL of online publication on the Official Gazette website or the court’s website> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Anayasa Mahkemesi [AYM] [Constitutional Court], July 5, 2018, E. 2018/100, K. 2018/79 (Turk.).

Optional citation format for decisions published in the Constitutional Court’s official reporter: <court name> [<abbreviation of court name>] [<English translation of court name>], <date of decision>, <matter number> <decision number>, <author of the individual opinion, if applicable>, <paragraph number, if applicable>, AMKD <volume number> (<issue number, if applicable>), <page number(s)> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Anayasa Mahkemesi [AYM] [Constitutional Court], Nov. 12, 2020, E. 2020/10, K. 2020/67, para. 15, AMKD 58(1), 239-40 (Turk.).

Since 2012, the Constitutional Court has issued individual complaint (bireysel başvuru) decisions. For individual complaint decisions, use the following citation format: <court name> [<abbreviation of court name>] [<English translation of court name>], <name of applicant>, App. No. <application number>, <date of decision>, <author of the individual opinion, if applicable>, <paragraph number, if applicable>, <optionally, URL of online publication on the Official Gazette website or the court’s website> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Anayasa Mahkemesi [AYM] [Constitutional Court], Dilan Dursun, App. No. 2015/18831, Nov. 2, 2022, para. 42, https://www.resmigazete.gov.tr/eskiler/2023/01/20230125-5.pdf (Turk.).

Court of Cassation

Türkiye’s Court of Cassation (Yargıtay) is the nation’s highest appellate court for both civil and criminal cases.

Citation format: <court name> [<abbreviation of court name>] [<English translation of court name>], <name of the chamber issuing the decision>, <date of decision>, <matter number> <decision number>, <author of the individual opinion, if applicable>, <paragraph number, if applicable>, <optionally and if available, URL of online publication on the Official Gazette website or the court’s website> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Yargıtay [Yarg.] [Court of Cassation], 4th Civil Chamber, Nov. 17, 1986, E. 1986/4898, K. 1986/7786 (Turk.).

Council of State

Türkiye’s Council of State (Danıştay) is the nation’s highest appellate court for administrative law cases, including tax law cases.

Citation format: <court name> [<abbreviation of court name>] [<English translation of court name>], <name of the chamber issuing the decision>, <date of decision>, <matter number> <decision number>, <author of the individual opinion, if applicable>, <paragraph number, if applicable>, <optionally and if available, URL of online publication on the Official Gazette website or the court’s website> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Danıştay [Danis.] [Council of State], 2nd Chamber, Jan. 19, 1996, E. 1994/2059, K. 1996/86 (Turk.).

Court of Jurisdiction Disputes

The Court of Jurisdictional Disputes (Uyuşmazlık Mahkemesi) acts as the final arbiter when there is a dispute over which court has jurisdiction and decides the court that will hear the case.

Citation format: <court name> [<abbreviation of court name>] [<English translation of court name>], <name of the chamber issuing the decision>, <date of decision>, <matter number> <decision number>, <author of the individual opinion, if applicable>, <paragraph number, if applicable>, <optionally and if available, URL of online publication on the Official Gazette website or the court’s website> (<country abbreviation if not evident from context>).

Uyuşmazlık Mahkemesi [Uyus. Mahk.] [Court of Jurisdictional Disputes], Civil Chamber, Sept. 24, 2018, E. 2018/530, K. 2018/467 (Turk.).

Constitution

Türkiye has had five constitutions since 1876: one in 1876, the second in 1921, the third in 1924, the fourth in 1961, and the fifth and current constitution in 1982. The current constitution of 1982 may be cited as follows:

Türkiye Cumhuriyeti Anayasası [Constitution of the Republic of Türkiye], Nov. 9, 1982, art. 104, para. 2, cl. a (Turk.).

Previous constitutions may be cited as follows:

Türkiye Cumhuriyeti Anayasası [Constitution of the Republic of Türkiye], July 9, 1961, art. 45 (Turk.).

Teşkilat-ı Esasiye Kanunu [Constitution of the Republic of Türkiye], Apr. 20, 1924, art. 104 (Turk.).

Teşkilat-ı Esasiye Kanunu [Organic Law of Türkiye], Jan. 20, 1921, art. 1 (Turk.).

Kanun-i Esasi [Constitution of the Ottoman Empire], Dec. 23, 1876, art. 55 (Turk.).
