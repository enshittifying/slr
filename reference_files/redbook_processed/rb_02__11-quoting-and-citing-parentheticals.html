1.1: Quoting and Citing Parentheticals

SLR always requires a quoting parenthetical when the quoted source is itself quoting another source. If the author has forgotten to include a quoting parenthetical, you are responsible for adding one yourself, which may involve locating and pulling the quoted source, citechecking it, and uploading it to Box. However, SLR never requires a citing parenthetical. If the author wants to include a citing parenthetical, they may do so, but you need not add one or ask the author to add one.

For quoting and citing parentheticals, you are responsible for citechecking the quoted or cited source just as if the author had cited it directly. Occasionally, checking the quoted source is impracticable because it is impossible to retrieve. If you add a quoting parenthetical but are unable to citecheck the quoted source, please be sure (1) to leave an [RCC] in the document and (2) to flag the issue for your SE.
