Redbook Section 1.2: Identifying References

1.2: Identifying References

When a citation is used solely to identify a particular authority—rather than to support a factual or legal proposition—the footnote call number should appear immediately after the source (and after any punctuation mark except for a dash or colon, see BB 1.1(a)), in the text. Examples:

Justice Brandeis never knew about Brown v. Board of Education1 or Between the World and Me;2 both were written after he died.
  1. 347 U.S. 483 (1954).
  2. Ta-Nehisi Coates, Between the World and Me (2015).

Please Note: When a source is introduced in a sentence but also supports its proposition, this rule does not apply. Include only a single footnote with the call number where it would otherwise appear.
