1.9: Nested Parentheticals

When an explanatory parenthetical has text that itself requires a parenthetical, the parentheticals should be nested. See BB 10.6.4. This rule applies to all types of sources, not only to cases. Example:

Devin M. Flynn, Why the Candidate Exercise Needs More Schitt's Creek, 76 Stan. L. Rev. 721, 736 (2024)
("'Th[ose] crows have eyes,' and they're looking for Honor Code violations." (alteration in original) (quoting The Crows Have Eyes 3: The Crowening (Meg Harrington dir., 2018))).
