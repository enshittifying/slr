18.8: Dates for Online PDFs

For PDFs (whether qualifying as a report or a miscellaneous document, see RB 18.7), include only the year on the document, even if there is more specific date information available. This is different from the rule for citations to webpages, where SLR includes all date and time information given on the page. Cf. RB 18.4. If there is no date available, use an (n.d.) parenthetical instead. Cf. BB 15.4(c). Do not include the archival date, and do not impute a date based on context clues (for example, the academic or fiscal year to which a document refers, which may not in fact be the date when the document was created). Examples:

Correct: Stanford L. Sch., Student Expense Budgets: 2018-2019, at 1 (2018), https://perma.cc/FXJ2 -VWEH.
Incorrect: Stanford L. Sch., Student Expense Budgets: 2018-2019, at 1 (Mar. 9, 2018), https://perma.cc /FXJ2-VWEH.
Correct:  Stanford  L.  Sch.,  Student  Expense  Budget:  2022-2023,  at  1  (n.d.),  https://perma.cc /V7U5-7TRY.
Incorrect: Stanford L. Sch., Student Expense Budget: 2022-2023, at 1, https://perma.cc /V7U5-7TRY (archived Aug. 11, 2022).

The same principles apply for PDFs cited as reports under BB 15.
