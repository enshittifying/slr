22.10: Capital Letter After a Colon

SLR follows a straightforward rule to determine whether to capitalize the first word after a colon: Capitalize if the word is a proper noun or the start of a complete sentence. Contra CMOS 6.63. Examples:

Correct: The Court was clear: No liability can attach without a proper showing of scienter.
Correct: The Court focused on two things: scienter and its role in proving liability.
