Redbook Section 4.4: Hereinafter

4.4: Hereinafter

If you think a "hereinafter" may be warranted, do not add it yourself. Instead, leave an [SE:] comment.

In general, editors tend to overestimate the need for hereinafters. There are only two circumstances in which inserting a hereinafter would be appropriate. First, according to BB 4.2(b), hereinafter should be used to differentiate between two sources by the same author that are both introduced in the same footnote—even if those sources are differentiated by formatting.

However, never introduce hereinafters that will never actually be used later. So, if only one work is cited again later in the article, only provide a hereinafter for that source. If neither source is cited again later, do not provide any hereinafters at all.

Finally, note that merely citing (as opposed to introducing) multiple sources by the same author in a single footnote does not call for the use of hereinafter. Example:

  1. See Taylor Swift, Monsters Turning into Trees, 43 U. Chi. L. Rev. 352, 357-59, 370 & n.56 (2014) [hereinafter Swift, Monsters] ("We were in screaming color."); see also Taylor Swift, King of Thieves 45, 47 (2010) [hereinafter Swift, Thieves] (citing Spider-Man 2 (Sam Raimi dir., 2004)); Taylor Swift, You're Losing Me, on Midnights (The Late Night Edition) (Republic Records 2023).
  2. Taylor Swift, Lover (Republic Records 2019).
  3. Compare Swift, Thieves, supra note 1, at 49 ("Karma's a relaxing thought "), and Swift, supra note 2, with Swift, Monsters, supra note 1, at 359 n.49 (reviewing statutes of limitations to determine whether tortfeasors are "in the clear yet"). But see Ariana Grande, Starbucks Confusion 27 (2020) (reflecting on the difficulty of ordering a venti latte).
  4. Grande, supra note 3, at 35; see also Swift, supra note 2.

Second, hereinafter is appropriate when a source is cited again later and its "short" form would otherwise be particularly unwieldy (that is, exceeding roughly one line in length). Example:

  1. Notice of Filing of Supplemental Exhibit to Debtors' Motion for Entry of (I) An Order (A) Approving Bidding Procedures & Bid Protections in Connection with the Sales of Certain of the Debtors' Assets, (B) Approving the Form & Manner of Notice, (C) Scheduling Auctions & a Sale Hearing, (D) Approving Procedures for the Assumption & Assignment of Contracts, & (E) Granting Related Relief & (II) An Order (A) Approving the Sale of Assets Pursuant to the Bidding Procedures, (B) Authorizing the Sale of Assets Free & Clear of Liens, Claims, Encumbrances, & Interests, (C) Authorizing the Assumption & Assignment of Contracts, & (D) Granting Related Relief at 7, In re Patriot Coal Corp., No. 15-32450 (Bankr. E.D. Va. June 23, 2015), ECF No. 385 [hereinafter Patriot Notice]; see also 1 U.S.C. § 1 (defining certain words).
  2. See Patriot Notice, supra note 1, at 15.
