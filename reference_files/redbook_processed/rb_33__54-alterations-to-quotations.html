Redbook Section 5.4: Alterations to Quotations

5.4: Alterations to Quotations

As a clarification to BB 5.2, do not alter the style of quoted material to conform to Redbook, Bluebook, or CMOS style rules. However, if the original source makes a clear error (such as a spelling or substantial grammatical error, but not merely a deviation from style rules), use "[sic]" to indicate that the error is simply a faithful reproduction of the original source. Example:

"Calvin and Hobbes were [sic] a great comic strip."

Basic formatting may be changed inside quoted material without notation. See RB 1.13. Use the following table as a guide for what items you should and should not change within a quotation:

Alter Without Comment Alter with Comment Do Not Alter
  • Ellipses (see BB 5.3)
  • Smart for straight quotes
  • En for em dashes or hyphens
  • Italics for underlining (see RB 2.1)
  • Italics for bolding when used for emphasis (see RB 2.2)
  • Formatting and layout not used for emphasis (see RB 1.13)
  • Spacing issues (such as spaces around dashes, see RB 22.6, the one- space rule, see RB 22.7, and nonbreaking spaces, see RB 22.8)
  • Changes in emphasis (see RB 1.13; BB 5.2(d))
  • Changes in capitalization to improve readability (see RB 1.13)
  • Initial capitalization (use brackets; see BB 5.2(a))
  • Misspellings or serious errors (use [sic])
  • Serial comma
  • Hyphenation
  • Numerals vs. words
  • Other capitalization issues
  • Alternate, but still correct, spellings (see RB 22.1)
