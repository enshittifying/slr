7.1: Italicizing Foreign Words

SLR follows a simple procedure for determining whether a non-English word or phrase has been "incorporated into common English usage," BB 7(b): Look up a word or phrase that does not appear in the examples in BB 7(b) in the online version of Merriam-Webster. If the word or phrase appears in the main dictionary, it has been incorporated into common English usage and need not be italicized. If the word or phrase does not appear in Merriam-Webster or appears only in its module for legal definitions (as with "sua sponte," for example), italicize it. Leave a comment for your SE if you have any doubts.
