10.9: The Five-Footnote Rule for Short Citations

For purposes of BB 10.9(a), citations (or valid short-form citations) to cases in quoting parentheticals, citing parentheticals, subsequent history, and briefs or other court filings (see BB 10.8.3) should be counted in determining whether the case appears in one of the preceding five footnotes and can thus be cited again in short form.

Id. references also count for purposes of the five-footnote rule.
