12.3: Short Forms for Statutes

To clarify BB 12.10(b), the short form for statutes can be used (subject to the five-footnote rule, see BB 10.9(a)) for citations to any section within the same title of the code, but the long form must be repeated for citations to different titles. Examples:

  1. 42 U.S.C. § 1983.
  2. See id. § 1981; 52 U.S.C. § 10301(a); Del. Code Ann. tit. 23, §§ 1101-1113 (2022); see also id. § 1708.
  3. See 42 U.S.C. § 1983; Del. Code Ann. tit. 9, § 2302 (2022).

However, SLR does not use a short form version (other than id.) that omits the title number or statute name.

Correct: 42 U.S.C. § 1983; National Environmental Policy Act § 102; id. § 103.
Incorrect: § 1983; § 102; § 103.
