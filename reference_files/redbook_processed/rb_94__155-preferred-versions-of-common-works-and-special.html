15.5: Preferred Versions of Common Works and Special Citation Forms

SLR’s preferred version of The Federalist is the 1961 edition edited by Clinton Rossiter. If you think there is a compelling reason to use a different version, please notify your SE. If the author cites a source which itself quotes or cites a different version of The Federalist, we would use that version in the citing or quoting parenthetical. See RB 1.8.

By SLR convention (and contrary to BB 15.10.1, ex. 8), a supra short-form citation to a Federalist essay should be formatted as follows:

The Federalist No. 51 (James Madison), supra note 17, at 319.

You may use a supra short-form citation to refer to any essay in a particular edition of The Federalist if the piece you are editing has previously cited any other essay from the same edition. For instance, the above example would be correct even if footnote 17 gave a long-form citation to The Federalist No. 23, written by Alexander Hamilton, rather than The Federalist No. 51.

SLR’s preferred version of Blackstone’s Commentaries is the last authorial edition: the London printing of the 12th edition (1793-95), available in SearchWorks through LLMC Digital.

SLR’s preferred translation of the Bible is the New Revised Standard Version unless there is a compelling reason to cite another translation (such as the King James Version).

SLR’s preferred version of the Code of Hammurabi is the 1904 translation by Robert Francis Harper. SLR has adopted the following citation format:

The Code of Hammurabi, King of Babylon § [sec no], at [page no] (Robert Francis Harper trans., 2d ed. 1904) (c. 2250 B.C.E.).

SLR’s preferred version of Paradise Lost is the 2021 edition by Gordon Teskey. Example:

John Milton, Paradise Lost IV.79-110, at 80 (Gordon Teskey ed., W.W. Norton & Co. 2d ed. 2020) (1674).

SLR has adopted the following citation form for Papal encyclicals:

Pope Paul VI, Encyclical Letter, Humanae Vitae ¶ 16 (July 25, 1968).
